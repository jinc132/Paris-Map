Paris Sight Map

Tourist Spots of Paris!


Purpose of Paris Sight Map

The Paris sight map was developed for the purpose of helping new travelers get some information on where the hot tourist spots are. For an individual who has never been to Paris, France this map is perfect as it pinpoints several locations where people have previously enjoyed the site. With Paris's rich history and art, it is difficult to determine where a person should start planning from and what they should see. So, I put together a sight map putting together. It includes a variety of diffferent tourist sites that is both popular and famous. All things considered, each aspect of the sight map was made for the purpose of providing people who are interested in visiting these tourist sites with a clearer picture and quick access to the right information. This information consists of the rating of each specific tourist spot along with a link to a website that previous tourists have posted reviews about. As well as a link to wikipedia page providing more in-depth about each tourist site. I wished to provided new tourists the much needed information about Paris's top tourist spots.


Reason for Design Choices

This particular basemap was chosen because it was the most subtle one while showing the necessary streets. It allows tourists and travelers to immediately notice the various tourist spots without the basemap's features cluttering the map and distracting the viewer. This basemap allows the viewer to have clear view of the attractions and streets which is the perfect tool for planning trips.

I have decided to separate large areas such as parks, gardens, and islands as polygons because there are a plethora of activities that take place in those specific location. They also are well known areas to provide the best view of paris and great for taking pictures. I decided to select the color orange for these polygons because it contrasts well against the basemap allowing the viewer to have a better view of the polygons.

I decided to use lines to differentiate bridges because they are paths and lines best show bridge path. I decided to show bridges because various bridges are carefully carved and each has a rich history to them. Some even have romantic myths connected to them which I believe is something each tourist should know about. Lines best shows when no path begins and ends and in this specific case bridges with special meanings are best represented by lines.

I selected the color purple for the bridge so that they are close to the color of the streets yet differs from them to make them pop. I have chosen to use markers to show musuems and historical landmarks because they clearly pinpoint the location of both sites distinctively. In order to differentiate museums from historical landmarks, I selected a musuem marker to represents all the famous museums in Paris and a star marker to represent historical landmarks. Since a star in this case represents the reputation and popularity of the sites. When either a polygon or a line feature is selected I have chosen to put in a image of the tourist spot so that viewers will know what to look for or what they will be looking at. The images will help them decide whether they wish to go visit the site or not. Both the polygon and marker features have popups that have hyperlinks in scenerio that tourists or people interested would like to have more information. I felt that people who are interested in visiting these sites required ample information so excluding the bridges, I have decided to show the name,address, ratings, and a brief description of each site.

I have found that by having all the layers on top of each other the viewer would not have a clear visual represention of the map or its tourist spots. They would not be able to access the information they need and it would look completely cluttered together. So, I chosen to put museums, historical landmarks, and tourist spots as three different buttons and exclude the bridges because it does not conflict with any of the other sites. This way tourists have a clear visual on what they want to see as well as clean visual of the tourist spots.


New Leaflet Skill

I have learned to create a customized icon marker. I have utilized this new skill to create the museum icon marker as well as the historical landmark icon marker. When the viewer selects a either a museum button or a historical landmark button the viewer can see several customized markers. When they click on the marker then, a popup will appear right above the marker providing the user with ample amount of information in regard to each tourist site. Tourist will clearly differentiate between which will show a museum and which will show a histoical landmark due to the marker design.

I have also learned to utilize the filter so that when I have two types of data I would be able to differentiate the data and show one set of data when I meets the right condition. I used filter to filter the tourist site data so that I could differentiate which is a museum and which is not. While likewise displaying them to the user. The user uses this feature when they are pressing either the museum button or the historical landmark button. When ever they are pressing those two buttons the filter is working and filtering the data.
